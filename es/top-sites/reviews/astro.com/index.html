History & Background

Founded
1983
Founders
Dr. Alois Treindl
Company
Astrodienst AG
Headquarters
Zollikon near Zurich, Switzerland

The seeds of Astrodienst were planted in 1979 when Alois Treindl, then pursuing his physics doctorate at ETH Zurich, coded his first astrology program on an HP 9845B computer. What began as an intellectual curiosity, the marriage of celestial mechanics and computational precision, evolved into something more substantial when he began selling color-printed charts in January 1980. The company crystallized as Astrodienst AG on October 4, 1983. Treindl's physics training proved essential: he developed PLACALC and later the Swiss Ephemeris, planetary calculation routines that would become the unseen foundation beneath most astrological software worldwide. A pivotal transformation arrived in 1987 when American astrologer and psychologist Liz Greene joined as primary author. Her collaboration wove psychological depth into the platform's mathematical precision, elevating Astrodienst from a calculation engine into a source of genuine astrological insight. The website www.astro.com launched in 1996, with the premium domain acquired in 1998. The Astro-Databank, containing over 60,000 verified chart records, found its permanent home within the platform in 2009, completing Astrodienst's transformation into the most comprehensive astrology archive online. Today, operated from a villa near Lake Zurich by approximately eight staff members, Astrodienst serves over 8 million monthly visitors across 12 languages. The founding vision endures: quality over mass-market appeal, using the ancient art of astrology to illuminate the depths of human nature.
