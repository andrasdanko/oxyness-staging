History & Background

Company

San Francisco, California, USA

Keen was founded in 1999 by Karl Jacob in San Francisco as a fee-based online advice platform called a "Live Answer Community." The concept originated during the dot-com boom, connecting users with experts via phone consultations. Major early investors included eBay, Microsoft, The Carlyle Group, and Benchmark Capital, with $60 million raised in 2000 and total funding reaching $109 million by year's end—making it the fastest-growing e-commerce company at the time. In 2002, Karl Jacob was ousted by the board, and the company was renamed Ingenio. It subsequently merged with LiveAdvice and Inforocket. Keen.com evolved into a brand focused specifically on psychic services. AT&T acquired Ingenio in 2007, and Alpine Investors purchased the company in 2013. From 2015-2023, Ingenio expanded through acquisitions of meditation apps and marketplace brands across Europe and North America. Today, Ingenio operates 20+ global brands across 6 languages, headquartered at 182 Howard Street #826, San Francisco. Current CEO is Laura Walsh. The platform has facilitated over 50 million conversations with over 10 million customer reviews, maintaining 1,600+ vetted advisors who undergo identity verification and screening processes.
