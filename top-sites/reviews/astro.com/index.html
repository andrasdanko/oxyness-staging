History & Background

Astrodienst's origins trace back to 1979 when Alois Treindl, then a physics graduate student at ETH Zurich, wrote his first astrology program on an HP 9845B computer. What began as an academic curiosity evolved into a pioneering enterprise when he started selling color charts in January 1980. The company was formally established as Astrodienst AG on October 4, 1983. Treindl's background in physics proved invaluable, as he developed PLACALC and later the Swiss Ephemeris - planetary calculation routines that would become the standard for astrological software worldwide. A transformative partnership began in 1987 when American astrologer and psychologist Liz Greene joined as the primary author. Her collaboration brought psychological depth to the platform's interpretations, elevating Astrodienst beyond mere calculation tools into a source of meaningful astrological insight. The website www.astro.com launched in 1996, and the premium astro.com domain was acquired in 1998. The Astro-Databank, containing over 60,000 verified chart records, was integrated in 2009, cementing Astrodienst's position as the most comprehensive astrology resource online. Today, operated from a villa near Lake Zurich by a small team of approximately eight staff members, Astrodienst serves over 8 million monthly visitors in 12 languages. The company maintains its founding philosophy of prioritizing quality over mass-market appeal, utilizing astrology to explore the deeper levels of human nature.
